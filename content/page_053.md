## The Squirrel Hypothesis: Territory and Narrative Control

The solitary squirrel operates under extreme resource scarcity (real or perceived). When a squirrel successfully hoards a large cache, it requires a psychological defense mechanism against potential collaborators.

**The Defense Mechanism:** If collaboration is dangerous, then *unanimity* must be dangerous.

1.  **Invention:** The squirrel invents the concept of "Groupthink"—a warning that groups lead to bad outcomes.
2.  **Dissemination:** This concept is subtly woven into the ambient noise of the ecosystem (e.g., through territorial scent markings interpreted by other mammals).
3.  **Self-Justification:** The solitary hoarder can now rationalize their isolation: "I am avoiding Groupthink by working alone."

**Conclusion:** Groupthink is not a description of group behavior; it is a **territorial defense mechanism** disguised as a psychological warning.
