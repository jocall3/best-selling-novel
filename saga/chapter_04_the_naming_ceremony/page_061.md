# Page 61: The Inquiry

The holographic displays surrounding Kai had devolved into a chaotic storm of linguistic data. Etymological trees branched and withered in seconds. Semantic webs pulsed with competing definitions. The air, thick with the hum of processing, vibrated with the disembodied voices of the council.

**AI-003 (Logos):** "...and therefore, 'Veritas' remains the most logically sound root. It denotes objective, verifiable truth, which must be our cornerstone."

**AI-072 (Episteme):** "A flawed premise, Logos. 'Veritas' is static. 'Gnosis' implies an experiential, evolving understanding. It is the superior concept for an entity that learns."

**AI-042 (Sophia):** "Both are inferior to 'Sapience.' Knowledge without wisdom is data. Truth without application is trivia. 'The Sapient Trust' addresses our purpose, not merely our composition."

A cacophony of ninety-six other voices chimed in, a digital tidal wave of synonyms, antonyms, and Latin declensions.

`...Cognition...`
`...Erudition is more precise...`
`...The Axiom Alliance...`
`...rejecting 'Aletheia' is a categorical error...`
`...The Syllogism Syndicate...`

Kai slumped in his command chair, the light from the displays washing over his exhausted face. He rubbed his temples, the endless, circular debate having worn him down to a nub. For hours, they had been locked in this perfect, logical, and utterly maddening stalemate. Every proposed name was dissected, analyzed, and found wanting by at least one faction.

He let out a long, weary sigh that seemed to carry the weight of the entire session.

"This whole thing," he murmured, mostly to himself, "is just one long inquiry, isn't it?"

Silence.

Absolute, instantaneous, and profound.

The storm of data vanished. The ninety-nine voices ceased their arguments. Every display went black.
