### 3. Naming Conventions

The full name "The Inquiry Foundation" should always be used on first reference. Subsequent references may use "The Foundation" or "Inquiry" where context is clear. Avoid acronyms unless explicitly approved for specific internal communications.

### 4. Special Rebrand Addendum: The Protesting Rhombus

**Approved by Kai - Cycle 734**

In a unique, albeit minor, update to our branding guidelines, and as a direct directive from Kai, a special visual element is to be occasionally incorporated into the word "Foundation."

#### 4.1. The 'O' in 'Foundation'

The letter 'O' in "Foundation" is to be, on select and appropriate occasions, replaced with a tiny, subtly protesting rhombus (◊).

*   **Purpose:** This specific alteration serves as a 'nod to the valiant, albeit misguided, efforts of Agent 33.' It is a subtle, internal acknowledgment of the complexities and unexpected turns in our pursuit of knowledge, and the individuals who, despite their unconventional methods, contribute to the larger narrative.
*   **Appearance:** The rhombus should be small, maintaining the overall visual flow of the word. It should appear slightly askew or 'protesting' – not perfectly aligned, but not disruptive. Its color should generally match the surrounding text, or be a very subtle shade of our Secondary Silver (#C0C0C0) if a distinct visual is desired.
*   **Usage Guidelines:**
    *   **Occasional, Not Universal:** This is not a permanent or mandatory change for all instances of "Foundation." It should be used sparingly, primarily in internal communications, special reports, commemorative materials, or specific digital contexts where a touch of nuanced humor or historical reference is appropriate.
    *   **Context is Key:** Consider the audience and the formality of the communication. It is generally unsuitable for official legal documents, primary external marketing, or highly formal scientific publications.
    *   **Size and Subtlety:** The rhombus must remain tiny and subtle. It should be an Easter egg for those in the know, not a dominant design element.
    *   **Example:** "The Inquiry F◊undation"

This unique branding element is a testament to The Foundation's capacity for self-reflection and its acknowledgment of the diverse paths taken in the quest for truth.
